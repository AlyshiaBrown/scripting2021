Economy

The main sources of income for Lethbridge come from agriculture, developing the city through construction, education and healthcare.

Many small businesses are in the downtown area, easily accessible through the transit system. Many of the city’s operating buses are stationed in the area, giving ease of access to these businesses and helping advertise the vast number of options available for consumers.

With an increase in demand for healthcare professionals, health providers like doctors and nurses have taken the stage as one of the leads in Lethbridge’s economy. Having most of the shares in the economy since 2001, healthcare remains one of the most important and valuable businesses in Lethbridge.

There are thousands of acres of farmland in southern Alberta, used for both crops like corn and wheat and livestock including horses and cattle. Alberta is the second largest agricultural province in Canada and earns 22% of Canada’s farming cash receipts.
